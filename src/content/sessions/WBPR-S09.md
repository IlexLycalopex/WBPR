## Block 3 — 01:05

**Caller:** Yes (rolled 4 — standard)
**Caller card:** Queen of Spades — *something witchy, occult*

Female. Age indeterminate. Her accent placed her nowhere specific. No ambient sound in the background — the wrong kind of empty, not silence but absence.

She said she'd been listening most nights. She called to ask whether the frost patterns on the east window repeated.

She knew about the east window. Oso hadn't mentioned it.

She has been cataloguing similar patterns: glass, still water, and one mirror she no longer owns. She believes a recurring shape is directional — that it indicates something rather than merely forms. She wanted to know if Oso had seen repetition.

Oso cited Herodotus. You can't step in the same river twice — time changes things, in the mind and in the world. Encouraged her to keep cataloguing.

*Herodotus,* she said. *Good. Most people give me Jung.*

She signed off cleanly. No static. No click.

Oso named her: the Collector.

The farmer in block one sat with me. Thirty-one years on the same land and now he's walking a house at night with nothing to do with his hands. Santaolalla got to him before I did. Sometimes the music does the work.

The Collector is a new one. She knew about the frost — not a guess, a confirmation. She asked with the precision of someone who already had the answer and wanted to see if I did too. The mirror she no longer owns is worth noting. The fact that she's been listening most nights is worth noting. The silence around her voice is worth noting most of all.
