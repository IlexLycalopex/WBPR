## Block 1 — 23:00

**Caller:** Yes (rolled 5 — urgent/distressed)
**Caller card:** Eight of Clubs — *knowledge they cannot unlearn*

Male voice, thirties. Fast, like he'd been rehearsing but had forgotten his place.

He'd found something on his work's internal system that he hadn't been looking for. A folder he'd opened by mistake, closed immediately. But not before he'd seen the names inside.

People he knew. Colleagues. A few friends.

He didn't say what the list was. He didn't need to. The shape of it was clear enough from how he talked around it.

*Once you've seen something like that,* he said, *what are you supposed to do with it?*

Oso said: document it if you can, somewhere safe and separate. Then put it down for tonight. You can't carry that on the first night.

He didn't sound convinced. He thanked Oso anyway.

He hung up before the block ended.

## Block 3 — 00:55

**Caller:** Yes (rolled 6 — phenomenon)
**Caller card:** Jack of Spades — *trickster entity, playful horror*

Female, age uncertain — mid-thirties, maybe older. Her tone was more puzzled than frightened. That was the telling detail.

She'd found a coin. Not unusual. But then she'd thrown it in a storm drain and found it again that afternoon, in the same jacket pocket. Same coin — she was certain because of a specific nick in the edge she'd noticed the first time. She threw it in a river. Found it again the next morning. She'd tried leaving it in a different state. It came back.

*It's not doing anything,* she said. *It just keeps coming back. Like it's trying to tell me something, but it only knows one word.*

Oso advised: don't try to read it as a message. Put it somewhere visible — don't hide it. See if the behaviour changes when it's acknowledged. Keep notes.

She said she'd try that.

The coffee pot activated during this call. Third time. Oso noted it without comment on air.

## Block 4 — 01:55

**Caller:** Yes (rolled 4 — standard)
**Caller card:** Seven of Hearts — *something bittersweet*

A quiet voice. Not sad — just at the end of something.

She said the year had been the longest she could remember. Not badly, just fully. More had happened than seemed possible to fit into twelve months. And tonight was the kind of night where you sit with all of it.

She asked if Oso ever felt like a broadcast was also a kind of goodbye.

Oso said: every time.

She said that was reassuring. Then she signed off.
